# Phase 4 — Review call script

30 minutes, screen-share, walking the client through the report. This is where ~half of clients
turn into paid implementation work. Don't rush to the upsell — deliver the value first, then ask.

**Email the report to the client BEFORE the call** so they've had time to read it. And keep the
deck ruthless — Corey iterated his twelve times, because *"a confused mind doesn't implement, a
confused mind doesn't get the ROI, and a confused mind doesn't upsell."*

## Structure (30 min)

1. **Recap the pain (2 min).** "Here's what I heard costing you the most time." Read back their
   own words. They feel understood before you prescribe anything.
2. **Walk the quick wins (10–15 min).** Slide by slide. For each tool: what it is, what it fixes,
   how long to set up, hours it saves. Show, don't tell — open the tool if you can. Corey spends
   ~50% of the whole call on the Recommended Solutions slide — that's where the value lands.
3. **The 4-day plan (5 min).** "You can do all of this yourself in about 10 minutes a day. Start
   Monday." This is the guarantee in action — most clients implement half the report in a week.
4. **The ROI slide (3 min).** Total hours back, total tool cost, monthly value. Let the number sit.
5. **What comes after (3 min).** Point at the higher-effort opportunities. "These aren't
   off-the-shelf — they're built. That's a separate conversation if you want it."
6. **The 3 closing questions (5 min).** ↓

## The 3 closing questions (the upsell trigger — Corey's exact phrasing)

Ask these plainly, then stop talking:

1. **"Of these recommendations, which is the most urgent for you?"**
2. **"Do you want to do this yourself, or would you like my help implementing some of these
   solutions?"**
3. **"What's your timeline — are these pain points killing you, or could you live another 60 days
   with them in place?"**

Roughly half say they'd rather have it done for them. That's the implementation build.

## The close

- **Mark up, then credit the fee.** Corey's actual tactic: price the build with the audit fee
  already added on, then "credit" the £750 against it — *"I'm still making the same amount of
  money, but psychologically they feel way better about it."* Keep it honest: the build price you
  quote must be a price you'd stand behind anyway; the credit is a framing device, not a lie about
  the discount.
- **Fix before automate.** If their process is broken, sell the process redesign first: *"If we
  just go and throw AI at your existing process, it is going to fall apart."* Current-state map +
  future-state blueprint is its own engagement; the automation build comes after.
- **Scope loosely, follow up in writing.** Don't invent a number on the call. "Let me put a short
  proposal together — two options, fixed price." Send it within 24 hours (draft for Chris to send).
- **Or the retainer.** If they have ongoing needs rather than one build: the AI Concierge —
  £900–£1,500/mo, two calls a month plus async, you build skills/automations as they go. Roster
  capped at 6, which is true and creates real scarcity.

## If they say no to the upsell

Fine. They still got a £750 report that saves them 5+ hours/week — that's a happy client and a case
study (with permission). Ask: **"Would you be open to me writing up what we did, anonymised if you
prefer? And do you know anyone else this would help?"** Referrals are the cheapest channel.

## Never

- Never send the proposal or follow-up email without Chris approving the specific message.
- Never invent testimonials or claim other clients you don't have.
- Never over-promise the build. Scope what you can actually deliver.
