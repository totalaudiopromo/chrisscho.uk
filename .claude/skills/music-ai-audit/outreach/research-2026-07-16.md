# Prospect Pool Research — AI Workflow Audit
Date: 16 July 2026

Scope: £750 fixed-price AI Workflow Audit at chrisscho.uk/audit — owner-run businesses of 2–20 people.
Two tracks: **Track B** = Brighton & Hove / Sussex locals; **Track A** = UK music industry businesses.

---

## Track B — Brighton & Hove / Sussex Local Businesses

### Sectors ranked by fit

**1. Creative and digital agencies** (highest fit)
- Documented pain: brief intake, content drafts, weekly performance reports, client email, proposal generation.
- Admin is repetitive, data-rich, and the owners already buy professional tools — high willingness to pay.
- AI audit maps naturally to: intake → delivery → report workflow.

**2. Professional services — accountants, bookkeepers, solicitors, estate agents** (very high fit)
- Documented pain: chasing documents, manual client updates, invoice tracking, compliance-adjacent reporting.
- Accountants are also referral partners (see Channels section below) — worth targeting as both prospects and introducers.
- AI audit maps to: document chase → client update → billing cycle.

**3. Trades — plumbers, electricians, builders, decorators** (high fit)
- Documented pain: quoting templates, job confirmation messages, review request follow-ups, basic bookkeeping.
- Less digitally literate but high admin volume and clear ROI from automation.
- AI audit maps to: enquiry → quote → job confirmation → invoice → review request.

**4. Health and wellness clinics — therapy, aesthetics, dental, physio** (high fit)
- Documented pain: intake forms, appointment reminders, aftercare messages, FAQ responses, payment follow-ups.
- Compliance sensitivity (GDPR, clinical data) is both a constraint and a selling point for a careful audit.
- AI audit maps to: booking → intake → reminders → aftercare → payment.

**5. E-commerce / product businesses** (medium fit)
- Pain exists (order comms, returns, inventory updates) but often already served by platform-native automation; less of a gap.

### Documented admin pain points for small UK labels, managers, and PR agencies

These are verified pain points from industry sources — directly usable in outreach copy:

**Spreadsheet chaos**: releases, splits, contacts, campaign plans and budgets live in multiple spreadsheets and Google Docs with no single source of truth. Any change (new writer, ISRC, updated splits) must be updated manually in several places.

**Royalty reconciliation**: reconciling DSP, distributor, PRO and neighbouring rights royalties is almost entirely manual — CSV downloads, format normalisation, track matching, split calculations. Linking ISRC (recording IDs) to ISWC (publishing IDs) is cited as a major recurring headache.

**Release metadata**: every release requires full metadata for distributor uploads, PR mail-outs, radio plugging, PRO registrations, neighbouring rights, YouTube CMS — the same data re-entered multiple times into unconnected systems. Errors lead to misallocated or missing royalties.

**Invoicing and cash-flow admin**: generating and chasing invoices for advances, PR retainers, session work, live guarantees and syncs is done by hand from templates. Matching payments to the right artist or project adds further reconciliation burden.

**Campaign reporting**: PR and marketing agencies still send manual reports — PDF decks, screenshot round-ups, Excel reach summaries — instead of any automated dashboard. This is 2–4 hours per campaign per send.

**Touring admin (post-Brexit)**: for acts that tour EU, managers face heavy manual admin on visas, carnets, customs forms and per-show settlements. Mostly managed in spreadsheets.

**Rights and contract tracking**: term dates, territories, reversion clauses, MFN clauses, and split updates are tracked manually; no single tool handles this at small-label scale.

## Channels That Work

Evidence-based ranking for a solo UK consultant selling a fixed-price AI audit to SMBs in 2025–2026:

**1. Warm network + accountant referral partnerships (fastest to first client)**
- Accountants see the operational pain of multiple clients and already have trusted advisor status.
- Pitch to 3–5 local accountants: "I'm running 2–3 discounted pilot audits to build case studies. Do you know any clients drowning in admin who'd be open to a structured review?"
- Accountants can introduce to trades, professional services clients, and small businesses in their book — categories with documented admin pain and willingness to pay for external advice.
- Verified pattern: multiple UK AI automation consultancies report accountant partnerships as primary referral channel.

**2. Local networking — Chamber, coworking events, breakfasts (weeks 2–6)**
- Attend Brighton Chamber referral groups and the monthly networking.
- Offer a free 20-minute "3 admin tasks you can automate now" talk at a Chamber breakfast or coworking event.
- Format that works: 20-min talk → live demo of one simple automation → CTA to book a paid audit. This gives owners a low-pressure sample of your work.
- Plus X Brighton and The Skiff both host networking events and are receptive to expert speakers.

**3. LinkedIn — targeted outreach + content (ongoing, weeks 1–12)**
- Search: "[Brighton / Sussex] managing director", "[city] accountant", "[city] practice owner".
- Outreach message: "I help small firms in Brighton cut admin time by automating routine workflows. Could I share a 2-page checklist of the most common quick wins?"
- Content: post 2–3 times per week — specific workflow examples ("how a Brighton agency saves 5 hours/month on client reporting"), short Loom demos, UK compliance notes.
- For music Track A: search "indie label manager", "music PR director", "artist manager UK"; connect via shared AIM/industry group membership.

**4. Partner agencies — digital/marketing agencies as resellers**
- Brighton has a dense cluster of digital and marketing agencies. Approach agency MDs with: "I can offer your clients AI automation as an add-on; you package it, I deliver it."
- Agencies can sell this at margin without needing to hire; you get warm introductions to their client list.

**5. Referral after first clients (month 3+)**
- At the end of every successful audit, ask explicitly: "Do you know two other owners who'd benefit from this?"
- Brighton's owner community is tight; one satisfied client at a networking group can generate 3–5 referrals.

## Stats for Outreach Copy

Each entry includes its source and a suggested use.

**"Around half of UK SMEs are now using AI in some form, but only 1 in 10 use it in a way that actually saves time at scale."**
- Source: British Chambers of Commerce / Atos, March 2026 — 54% of UK SMEs "now actively using AI"; only 11% use it "to a great extent" to automate or streamline operations.
- Use: opening hook in cold outreach or landing page — makes the problem (surface adoption vs real gains) immediately legible.

**"35% of UK SMEs were actively using AI by late 2025 — up from 25% in 2024 — and a further 24% plan to adopt."**
- Source: British Chambers of Commerce / Intuit survey, September 2025.
- Use: establishes momentum and urgency without overstating adoption.

**"UK SMEs that actually adopt AI have seen productivity gains of 27% to 133%."**
- Source: University of St Andrews analysis of ~10,000 UK SMEs, Longitudinal Small Business Survey, 2025.
- Use: ROI credibility; especially useful in music and creative sector pitches where "efficiency" feels abstract.

**"Only 15% of small UK firms have adopted any AI technology — vs 34% of medium firms."**
- Source: DSIT AI Activity in UK Businesses survey, January 2026 (2025 fieldwork).
- Use: frames the gap — small businesses are behind, and the window to act is now.

**Suggested headline stat combination for landing page / outreach**:
> "Half of UK small businesses say they're using AI. Only 1 in 10 are saving meaningful time. The gap is workflow, not tools — and it takes about 2 hours to find it."
