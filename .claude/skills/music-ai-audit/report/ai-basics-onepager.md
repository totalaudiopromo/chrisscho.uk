# Getting started with AI — a plain-English primer

*A companion to your AI Workflow Audit. Your report tells you **which** tools to use and why. This
page tells you **how** to use them well — no jargon, nothing you need a tech background for.*

---

**What AI actually is, for a business like yours.** Forget the robots-taking-over stuff. For you,
these are just tools that do the repetitive typing, drafting and looking-things-up that eats your
week — enquiry replies, first drafts, tidying a spreadsheet, summarising a long email thread. You
give them a job in plain English; they do the boring 80% in seconds; you keep the judgement. Your
report lists the specific ones worth your time.

**The one habit that makes them work: give them context.** A vague ask gets a vague answer. Tell
the tool *who it is, what you want, and who it's for.*

> Weak: "Write a reply to this email."
> Strong: "You're my label's assistant. Reply to this promoter's enquiry — friendly but brief,
> under 80 words, and ask which release they're interested in."

The second one is usable straight away. The first isn't. That single difference is 90% of the game.

**The first answer is a draft, not the final.** If it's not right, just say so — "make it shorter",
"more formal", "sound more like me". You're not stuck with what it hands back; you steer it.

**Four things to never do** (this is the part that keeps you safe):

1. **Don't paste in anything you couldn't email a stranger** — unreleased tracks, signed contracts,
   customer or fan personal details. Free tools may reuse what you type. When in doubt, leave it out.
2. **Assume it can be confidently wrong.** These tools sometimes invent names, numbers and dates
   that *look* right. Check anything factual — especially money, legal or tax — before you rely on it.
3. **Keep a human on anything that goes out under your name.** Read it, edit it, then send. It drafts;
   you approve.
4. **Don't pay for a tool until you've felt it save you time.** Your report shows roughly what each
   costs — start with the free tier, prove it earns its keep, then upgrade.

**How to start this week.** Don't try to do everything. Your report has a **4-day quick-start** — one
tool, one task per day, easiest first. Do that and nothing else for the first week. Momentum beats
ambition here.

**What "working" realistically looks like.** Not magic — just small, repetitive jobs done in seconds
instead of minutes, several times a day. Across a week that adds up: the audit's target is **7+ hours
a week** handed back to you.
