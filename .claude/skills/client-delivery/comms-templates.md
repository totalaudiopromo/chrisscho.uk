# Client delivery — draft comms templates

Draft copy for each stage of the implementation build (see `SKILL.md`). **All draft-only** — Chris
reviews and sends every one. UK spelling, GBP, calm tone, **name-only greeting** ("Sarah," not "Hi
Sarah,"), no emojis, no hype. `[square brackets]` = fill per client from the delivery tracker.

---

## Stage 1 — Proposal / lightweight SOW (to sign on)

> Sarah — good to get going. Here's the plan on one page so we're agreed before I start.
>
> **What I'm building:** [one-line outcome, e.g. an enquiry-reply assistant that drafts a first
> response to every booking email within a minute].
> **Deliverables:** [bullet list — the actual things they get].
> **Not included (so it's clear):** [out-of-scope bullets].
> **Timeline:** [X] working days from deposit and access.
> **Investment:** £[total]. Your £750 audit fee comes off that, so £[net]. 50% to start, 50% on
> handover.
> **Changes:** two rounds of tweaks are included; anything genuinely new I'll price as a small add-on
> so there are no surprises either way.
>
> If that looks right, reply "happy to go ahead" and I'll send the deposit invoice and a short
> checklist of what I need from you. We could be started by [day].

---

## Stage 2 — Welcome (once signed)

> Sarah — thanks, and welcome aboard. Here's exactly what happens next so nothing's a mystery:
>
> 1. I've attached a short checklist — the logins and bits I need to start. Sooner you get it back,
>    sooner I start.
> 2. We'll do a 30-minute kickoff on [date/option] — no building, just making sure I'm solving the
>    right problem.
> 3. Then I build, and you'll get a short update from me every week until it's done.
>
> One question to get us pointed the right way: **when this is finished, what would make it feel
> genuinely worth it for you?** Be as specific as you can — that's the bar I'll hold myself to.

## Stage 2 — Intake + access checklist

> To start on [build], I'll need:
>
> - **The tools involved:** [e.g. the inbox, the booking system, the spreadsheet] — access, or a
>   15-minute screen-share where you grant it. Whichever you prefer.
> - **A real example** of the thing we're automating — a recent [enquiry / release / invoice] so I
>   build against reality, not a guess.
> - **Anything it should sound like you:** a few past replies, your tone, any dos and don'ts.
> - **Who else touches this** — anyone whose sign-off or logins I'll need.
>
> Send passwords only via [agreed secure method] — never plain email. Where you can grant access to
> your own account instead of sharing a password, even better.

---

## Stage 3 — Kickoff call agenda (send ahead, run on the call)

> Quick agenda for our 30 minutes — nothing to prepare beyond turning up:
>
> 1. **The outcome** — I'll play back what I'm building in one sentence; you tell me if I've got it.
> 2. **How it works today** — walk me through the current [process] as you actually do it, clicks and
>    all. This is the important bit.
> 3. **The win** — we lock the one thing that makes this worth it (your answer from the welcome email).
> 4. **How we'll work** — weekly updates from me, same-day replies to your questions, and I'll flag
>    anything that slips the moment it does.
> 5. **Anything in the way** — approvals, other people, deadlines I should know about.

---

## Stage 5 — Weekly update (during the build)

> Sarah — this week's update on [build]:
>
> - **Shipped:** [what now works].
> - **Next:** [what I'm on this coming week].
> - **From you:** [one specific thing, or "nothing needed — all yours to ignore"].
>
> Short Loom here if you want to see it moving: [link].

---

## Stage 6 — Handover note (with the operator handbook + Loom)

> Sarah — it's built and it's yours. Attached:
>
> - **The operator handbook** — what it does, how to run it, and what to do if it ever misbehaves.
> - **A short walkthrough Loom** you can re-watch any time: [link].
>
> On our handover call I'll take you through it live and let you drive it once, so it's genuinely
> yours and not a black box. Against what you said would make this worthwhile — [their win] — here's
> how it measures up: [one honest line].
>
> Final invoice to follow after the call. Any wobble in the first 30 days, that's what our check-in
> is for — it's already booked.

---

## Stage 7 — 30-day check-in (booked at handover)

> Sarah — 30 days in. Quick 30 minutes to make sure [build] is still earning its keep?
>
> Two things I'd love to pin down: is it doing what we set out to do, and roughly what it's given you
> back — [hours a week / enquiries handled / time to reply]. If anything's drifted, we sort it on the
> call.
>
> And honestly — there's always a next bottleneck once the first one's gone. If it'd help to keep
> working through them together, I'll show you how the Concierge works. No pressure either way; the
> system you've got is yours regardless.
